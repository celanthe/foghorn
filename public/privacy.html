Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 30, 2026

Foghorn is a grief processing tool. The data it handles is deeply personal, and we treat it that way. Here is exactly what happens with your information.

What Foghorn stores

When you use Foghorn, the following data is saved on your device:

  • Ritual timestamps (when you recorded a ritual)
  • Grief intensity (a number from 1 to 10, self-reported)
  • Loss type (person, relationship, self, place, or multiple)
  • Grief phase (active, processing, integration, memorial)
  • Your name (as entered during onboarding)
  • Field notes (free-text reflections you write)
  • Weather data associated with each ritual (condition, temperature, wind, humidity)
  • Quarterly reflection scores and notes

Where your data lives

All of this data is stored locally on your device using IndexedDB and localStorage. It never leaves your device. There is no server, no account, no cloud sync. Foghorn does not have a backend that receives your data.

The one exception: weather and location

To show you the current weather, Foghorn asks for your geographic location (latitude and longitude) through your browser. If you grant permission, that location is sent to the OpenWeatherMap API to fetch weather data. This is the only data that leaves your device.

Foghorn does not store your location on any server. OpenWeatherMap receives your coordinates to fulfill the weather request; their own privacy policy governs how they handle that data.

You can decline the location prompt. Foghorn will fall back to a default location, or you can set a manual location in Settings.

No cookies, no analytics, no tracking

Foghorn does not use cookies. There are no analytics scripts, no tracking pixels, no fingerprinting, no telemetry of any kind. We genuinely do not know who uses this app or how.

Your data, your control

You can export all of your data as JSON or CSV from the Journal view at any time. You can delete all stored data from your browser's developer tools by clearing site data, or by uninstalling the app.

Sensitive data acknowledgment (GDPR Art. 9)

Grief intensity, loss type, grief phase, and reflection scores are sensitive personal data under GDPR Article 9 (data concerning health and emotional wellbeing). Foghorn stores this data exclusively on your device. It is never transmitted to any server, never processed remotely, and never shared with anyone. You are the sole controller of this data.

Third-party services

  • OpenWeatherMap - receives your latitude and longitude to return weather data. No other personal information is sent. Their privacy policy.
  • Open-Meteo - receives latitude, longitude, and date ranges for historical weather matching. No personal information beyond coordinates. Their terms.
